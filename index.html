Three-minute Training

【22】Halloween began long ago in Celtic lands. People had a festival called Samhain on October 31. They thought spirits could visit on that night. So they lit fires and wore simple masks to protect their homes. Later, Romans added harvest customs to Samhain. In the 7th century, the Christian church made November 1 All Saints’ Day. The night before became All Hallows’ Eve, or Halloween. Traditions slowly mixed. People carved turnips first, and later pumpkins, to make jack-o’-lanterns. Children dressed up and went to houses to ask for sweets. This became trick-or-treat. In the 19th century, immigrants carried these customs to North America. In the 20th century, towns held parties and games. Shops sold costumes and candy. Movies and stories made Halloween feel spooky and fun. Today, Halloween is a happy celebration in many countries. People enjoy costumes, pumpkins, and candy, but the old ideas from Samhain are still part of the holiday.

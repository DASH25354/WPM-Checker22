Three-minute Training

【9】Japanese bacon often does not get crispy. It has more water and less salt, and it is cut thicker. Because of this, it steams in the pan and cooks like ham. The fat does not melt well, so the meat stays soft. In the US and Europe, bacon is cured and smoked longer. It has more salt and fat, and it is sliced thin. When you cook it, water is low, so it fries fast and becomes crisp. Some people add a little sugar to help browning. To make Japanese bacon crisp, cut it thin, pat it dry with paper, cook on medium heat, and use a rack to drain fat. Cooking in the oven on a rack also helps it get crunchy.
